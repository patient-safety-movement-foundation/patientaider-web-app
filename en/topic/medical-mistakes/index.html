Signs of a Rapidly Declining Patient1234567891011121314

  • Shortness of breath, gasping, panting and/or fast breathing
  • Very slow breathing
  • Drop in blood pressure
  • Becomes very pale
  • Cold sweats, fever or chills
  • Confusion, dizziness, &/or acting strangely
  • Increase or decrease in heart rate
  • Extreme pain
  • Swelling of the face and hands
  • Low urine output
  • Seeing spots, flashing lights, blurring or unable to see
  • Bleeding from gums, nose, eyes or in urine &/or bowel movements
  • Seizures 
  • High or low temperature
  • Changes in heart rate
  • Patient doesn’t look right

Prevention of Complications:67891011121314

  • Recognizing abnormal vital signs
  • Recognizing abnormal lab results
  • Early warning system of monitors (pulse oximetry, heart rate, blood pressure & capnometry)
  • Rapid response to patient’s declining condition
  • Rapid & open communication and "huddles" between medical staff
  • Rapid treatment of complication
  • Family participation in nurse and doctor bedside reports
  • Nurses & families alerting Rapid Response Team
  • Patient and family engagement

Reasons Why Patients Could Fail:67891011121314

  • Poor communication between healthcare providers
  • Understaffing
  • Outdated communication technologies
  • Hierarchical barriers between medical staff
  • Not recognizing a deteriorating patient
  • Incomplete handoffs between healthcare providers
  • Not responding rapidly to an impending or ongoing crisis
  • Medical staff not engaging with patients and families

If Something Goes Wrong:

1. Speak with someone right away. If you believe you have experienced a medical error or if you would like to report a high-risk situation, please contact the hospital's ombudsman, or the designated person responsible for discussing and investigating complaints and reports, before contacting CMS or the public health governing body.15

2. Ask respectfully for an honest and clear explanation from the healthcare provider.1516

3. Ask for an investigation into the mistake and a family meeting to explain the results.1516

4. Ask for the Patient Advocacy, the Patient & Family Relations and/or the Risk Management department for help.1516

5. If needed after the discussion with the ombudsman, contact your state's public health services. Find your State's Health Department17 For Medicare patients contact Medicare at 1-800-MEDICARE or How to file a complaint (grievance) on Medicare website17

6. Contact the Joint Commission at 1-800-994-6610 or The Joint Commission's Report a Patient Safety Event17

7. Doctors and nurses are ethically bound to disclose medical errors.1819
