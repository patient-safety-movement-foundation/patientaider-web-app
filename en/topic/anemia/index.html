Anemia & Blood Transfusions

Blood

Blood is a fluid in your body made up of 4 parts:1

  • Red blood cells- have hemoglobin (Hgb) that carries oxygen through the body
  • Plasma-liquid that carries the red blood cells
  • White blood cells-fight infection
  • Platelets-help the blood to clot

Normal hemoglobin is >13 Hgb for men and >12 Hgb for women.2

Anemia

Anemia is when you don’t have enough healthy red blood cells.3

Causes of anemia:1234

  • Blood loss
  • Not making enough red blood cells
  • High loss of red blood cells
  • Red blood cells not working
  • Low level of hemoglobin
  • Low intake &/or absorption of iron
  • Low intake &/or absorption of vitamins A, B-12, folic acid, riboflavin and copper
  • Pregnancy
  • Genetic blood disorders
  • Growth

The main cause of anemia is iron deficiency. This is the most common type of anemia.2

Symptoms of anemia:1234

  • Feeling weak or tired
  • Fast or irregular heartbeat
  • Chest pain
  • Pounding in your ears
  • Headaches
  • Difficulty thinking
  • Dizziness
  • Pale skin
  • Shortness of breath
  • Cold hands or feet

Risks for anemia:1234

  • Surgery
  • Long hospital stay, especially in the ICU
  • Multiple lab tests
  • Kidney disease
  • Cancer
  • Liver disease
  • Thyroid disease
  • Pregnancy
  • Heavy menstrual periods
  • Diet low in iron, folic acid, or vitamin B12
  • Rheumatoid arthritis

Treatments for anemia:12345

  • Reduce blood draws for lab tests
  • Iron given by IV &/or pills
  • Replace vitamins and folic acid
  • Erythropoietin
  • Transfusion of red blood cells (RBC)

Blood Transfusion

Blood transfusion is when blood or a part of blood is given through an IV (intravenous line) into a vein.

Risks of blood transfusion:456789

  • Wrong blood type given
  • Wrong patient given transfusion 
  • Reaction to transfusion (acute & delayed)
  • Transmission of disease in blood
  • Acute lung injury
  • Fluid overload
  • Changes to the patient's immune system
  • Infection

Questions to Ask:

  • Why do I have to get a blood transfusion?
  • What are the risks if I get this transfusion?
  • What are the alternatives to a blood transfusion?
