COVID-19

COVID-19 is a new disease caused by a coronavirus that is spread from one person to another. This illness can range from mild to very serious symptoms and even death.1

Symptoms:2

  • Fatigue
  • Fever &/or chills
  • Shortness of breath
  • Constant cough
  • Headache
  • Loss of smell or taste
  • Body aches
  • Sore throat
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Stuffy or runny nose

  • Trouble breathing
  • Constant chest pain or pressure
  • New confusion
  • Difficulty waking up or staying awake
  • Bluish lips or face

Adults at Higher Risk:3

  • 65 years and older
  • Cancer
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Obesity
  • Pregnancy
  • Smoking
  • Sickle cell disease
  • Diabetes
  • Heart disease
  • Lung disease

Children at Higher Risk:3

  • Obesity
  • Sickle cell disease
  • Asthma
  • Congenital heart disease
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Diabetes
  • Severe neurologic disease
  • Inherited metabolic disorders

  • Severe genetic disorders

Prevention:4

  • Wear a mask over your nose and mouth.
  • Stay at least 6 feet away from other people who don’t live with you.
  • Avoid crowds.
  • Avoid poorly ventilated indoor spaces.
  • Wash your hands often with soap & water.
  • Cover coughs & sneezes.
  • Clean & disinfect frequently touched surfaces.
  • Monitor your health daily.

  • Get the COVID-19 vaccination. Use Vaccine Finder to find COVID-19 vaccines in your area.

Testing:5

  • If you have COVID-19 symptoms.
  • If you have been in close contact with someone who has COVID-19.
  • If you have been in crowded indoor &/or outdoor places.

  • If your healthcare provider, local or state health department has asked you to get a test.

    Embedded content: https://youtu.be/K-zS-2KiBmk

What to Do If You Have COVID-19:6

  • Stay at home.
  • Stay away from other people & pets in your home.
  • Wear a mask over your nose and mouth when you are with other people & pets.
  • Monitor your symptoms.
  • Call ahead before visiting your healthcare provider.
  • Cover your mouth & nose when you cough or sneeze.
  • Wash your hands often for 20 seconds with soap & water.
  • Don’t share personal household items.
  • Clean frequently touched surfaces in your room (& separate bathroom if available).
  • Even if you don’t feel sick, you can still spread COVID-19.

Questions to Ask if Your Loved One is on a Ventilator7:

  • When can they breathe on their own?
  • When can the sedation medication be reduced?
  • Does the patient have pressure sores on their back and/or buttocks?
  • Can the patient have a subglottic secretion drainage endotracheal tube if the ventilator will be in more than 72 hours?
  • Are the patient's teeth &/or dentures being cleaned twice a day?
  • Is the patient's mouth being rinsed with a non-alcohol based mouth rinse?
  • If the patient is at risk for aspiration will their teeth & mouth be cleaned 2-4 times a day with a suction toothbrush kit?
  • Is the patient being suctioned every 4 hours?
  • Is the patient’s head of the bed greater than 30 degrees?
  • Is everyone that sees the patient washing their hands?
  • Does the patient have a fever?
  • Is the patient feeling sick to their stomach?
  • Is the patient feeling sudden pain?
  • Does the patient have redness, swelling or tenderness at the airway insertion site?

Guide to Medical Care at a Distance

Vaccines:8

Vaccines are one of the best ways to prevent getting COVID-19. Vaccines teach your body how to fight the COVID-19 virus.

  • Studies show the vaccines are safe and effective.
  • Vaccines do not contain live virus
  • Gives you better protection than getting sick with COVID-19.
  • You can get COVID-19 again even if you’ve had it.
  • Vaccines will not change your DNA.
  • Side effects show that the vaccine is working to build your immunity to COVID-19.
  • Most people with medical conditions should get the vaccine.
  • You must keep taking steps to prevent COVID-19 after getting the vaccine.

Side Effects of Vaccine:9

  • Pain and discomfort at the injection site
  • Swelling of the skin near injection site
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Body chills
  • Headache

Ways to Help Side Effects:9

  • Put a clean, cold, & wet cloth over the injection site
  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Dress lightly
  • Rest

  • Ask your doctor about these over-the-counter medications:

    • Acetaminophen
    • Ibuprofen
    • Aspirin
    • Antihistamines
  • Don’t take these medications before getting the vaccine.
  • Call your doctor if the side effects get worse after 24 to 48 hours.

Common Questions and Answers:9

Where can I get the vaccine?

  • Use Vaccine Finder to find COVID-19 vaccines in your area.

What are the side effects from the vaccine?

  • Pain and discomfort at the injection site
  • Swelling of the skin near the injection site
  • Fever
  • Fatigue
  • Body chills
  • Headache

If I’m pregnant can I get the vaccine?

  • Yes, there is no evidence that fertility is affected by the COVID-19 vaccine.
  • CDC Information About COVID-19 Vaccines for People Who Are Pregnant or Breastfeeding

Do I need to get the vaccine if I’ve had COVID-19?

  • Yes, because no one knows how long the protection from the COVID-19 infection will last.
  • You can get COVID-19 again even if you’ve had it.
  • If you were infected with COVID-19 and received the monoclonal antibodies or convalescent plasma, it is advised that you wait 90 days before receiving a COVID-19 vaccine.
  • Ask your doctor about any treatments you may have received when you had COVID-19 and if any of these may affect receiving a COVID-19 vaccine.

After getting the vaccine, do I need to wear a mask, wash my hands, and social distance?

  • Yes, you will need to wear a mask, keep 6 feet from others, wash your hands, & avoid crowds inside with poor ventilation.

If I am sick with the COVID-19 infection, can I still receive a COVID-19 vaccination?

  • No. If you are sick with the COVID-19 infection, wait to receive a COVID-19 vaccine until you are fully recovered (this includes meeting the criteria for discontinuing self isolation).

Can I still receive a COVID-19 vaccine with medical conditions?

  • Individuals with medical conditions are able to receive a COVID-19 vaccine, but they should not have had an immediate or severe allergic reaction to a COVID-19 vaccine or any ingredients in the vaccine.

What are the benefits of the vaccine?

  • COVID-19 vaccines produce protection from infection by allowing the body to develop an immune response to the virus that causes COVID-19.
  • By developing an immune protection to the SARS Cov2 virus, the chance of getting the COVID-19 infection or developing severe symptoms upon infection are much less.
  • This immunity will help to fight the infection.
  • Getting vaccinated can help you, your loved ones, and your community to stop the spread of COVID-19.

How long will the COVID-19 vaccine protection last?

  • Experts currently do not know how long protection will last. Receiving a vaccination is a safer choice for decreasing infection for you, your loved ones, and your community.

Coping with Stress:10

  • Take breaks from the news and social media.
  • Eat well.
  • Care for yourself.
  • Walk, exercise, meditate, & listen to music.
  • Stay connected with others.
  • Get enough sleep.
  • Relax by doing activities you enjoy.
  • Avoid excessive alcohol, tobacco, & substance abuse.
  • Call your healthcare or mental health provider if you are struggling with stress.
