Clostridioides difficile Infection (C. diff)

An infection caused by the Clostridioides difficile bacterium. 2

Embedded content: https://youtu.be/E3AspGb-QA4

Symptoms

  • Diarrhea
  • Fever
  • Belly pain
  • Nausea
  • Loss of appetite
  • Frequent bowel movements

Prevention

Healthcare providers and visitors must wash their hands with soap and water and wear gloves, gowns, and/or masks (Isolation Precautions) when caring for patients who have a C. diff infection.

Soap and water are more effective at hand cleaning than hand sanitizer with a C. difficile infection.3

1. Use disinfectant wipes on all surfaces that you or the patient will come in contact with. Ask the nurse for these.4

2. Put up a sign telling staff to wash their hands with soap & water on your door and above your bed.4

3. Make sure doctors and nurses wipe the round end of the stethoscope with an alcohol wipe.4

Embedded content: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M8AKTACyiB0

4. Gloves need to be worn when there is contact with blood or other body fluids, mouth, nose, lungs, urinary and digestive tracts, open skin areas, or items that are soiled with blood or other body fluids. Healthcare workers need to wash their hands both before and after using gloves. Wearing gloves is never a substitute for hand washing!5 Gloves can have microscopic tears and holes.

5. The patient must wash their hands with soap & water before eating every meal & before going to sleep.5

6. The patient needs toothbrushing, &/or denture cleaning, & mouth rinsing 2-4 times every day.1

7. Deep breathing, incentive spirometer & coughing exercises every hour while awake. The patient should do at least 10 deep breaths every hour using the incentive spirometer while in bed.6

8. When is the soonest the patient can safely get out of bed & walk?1

9. Cellphones are dirty78

  • Wipe your cell phone with an alcohol wipe.
  • Do not take a cell phone into the bathroom.
  • Do not place the cell phone on the floor, especially the bathroom floor.

10. Cover your mouth when you cough or sneeze. Use your elbow or arm instead of your hands.

11. All visitors need to:5

  • Wash their hands with soap & water before entering and leaving the room
  • Do not visit if they feel ill
  • Avoid using the patient’s bathroom
  • Follow isolation precautions
