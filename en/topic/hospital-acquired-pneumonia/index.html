Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia

An infection of the lungs that happens in the hospital. 10

Symptoms:

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Cough with phlegm
  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Trouble breathing 10

Guide to Medical Care at a Distance

All healthcare providers and visitors must wash their hands. The average handwashing rate is 40% for healthcare providers in the hospital.4 Never be afraid to ask your doctor or nurse to please wash their hands!

Embedded content: https://youtu.be/tZnKlFtAUDw

  • Hand washing by everyone
  • Swallow assessment for aspiration risk (aspiration: something enters the airway or lungs by accident)
  • Oral care assessment by a nurse
  • Toothbrushing & mouth rinsing 2-4 times a day
  • Denture cleaning/soaking & mouth rinsing 2 times a day. Remove when sleeping.
  • 24 hr. suction toothbrush kit for aspiration risk patients
  • Head of the bed raised more than 30 degrees
  • Deep breathing, incentive spirometer & coughing exercises every hour while awake.
  • The patient should do at least 10 deep breaths every hour using the incentive spirometer while in bed.
  • When is the soonest the patient can safely get out of bed & walk?
  • Patients with feeding tubes need assessment every 4 hours

Incentive Spirometer Use Information from Medline Plus

Patient on a ventilator11131415:

Embedded content: https://youtu.be/4rFjAmaDXlg

  • Ask every day to see if the patient can breathe without the ventilator.
  • Head of bed has to be greater than 30 degrees at all times
  • Tell the nurse if the patient slides down in the bed.
  • Ask about reducing the amount of sedation medication called a "sedation vacation" every day.
  • Watch for bed sores(pressure ulcers) on the patient's lower back and buttocks.
  • When is the soonest that the patient can get safely out of bed? 3
  • Are the patient's teeth &/or dentures being cleaned twice a day?
  • Is the patient's mouth being rinsed with a non-alcohol based mouth rinse?
  • If the patient is at risk for aspiration will their teeth & mouth be cleaned 2-4 times a day with a suction toothbrush kit?
  • Is the patient being suctioned every 4 hours?
  • Can the patient have a subglottic secretion drainage endotracheal tube if the ventilator will be in more than 72 hours?

Embedded content: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l4HpcOpQaoQ

1. Bring in hand sanitizer for the patient, healthcare providers, and visitors to use. Ask your doctor or nurse to wash their hands with you.

2. Use disinfectant wipes on all surfaces that you or the patient will come in contact with. Ask the nurse for these.5

3. Put up a sign telling staff to wash their hands on your door and above your bed.2

Embedded content: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M8AKTACyiB0

4. Make sure doctors and nurses wipe the round end of the stethoscope with an alcohol wipe.5

5. Gloves need to be worn when there is contact with blood or other body fluids, mouth, nose, lungs, urinary and digestive tracts, open skin areas, or items that are soiled with blood or other body fluids. Healthcare workers need to wash their hands both before and after using gloves. Wearing gloves is never a substitute for hand washing!2 Gloves can have microscopic tears and holes.

6. Healthcare providers and visitors need to follow Isolation Precautions of wearing gowns, gloves and sometimes masks if the patient's door has an Isolation Precaution sign on it.

Isolation Precaution Information from the National Library of Medicine

7. The patient must wash their hands before eating every meal and before going to sleep.2

8. Cellphones are dirty.67

  • Wipe your cell phone with an alcohol wipe.
  • Do not take a cell phone into the bathroom.
  • Do not place the cell phone on the floor, especially the bathroom floor.

9. Cover your mouth when you cough or sneeze. Use your elbow or arm instead of your hands.

10. When a nurse is giving IV medications, make sure the nurse cleans the IV port for 15 seconds before and after with an alcohol wipe.8 Don’t touch where your IV lines meet your skin.9

11. Tell your nurse and doctor if there is pain, swelling, redness, itching or wetness at an IV line or incision.2

Embedded content: https://youtu.be/67vDXQMxUz4

12. Ask if you or the patient can have their own blood pressure cuff to be used only by them.2

13. Remove your slippers worn over socks before getting back into your bed. This prevents germs from the floor coming into your bed.2

14. All visitors need to:2

  • Wash their hands before entering and leaving the room
  • Do not visit if they feel ill
  • Avoid using the patient’s bathroom
