Ask Questions! 

The nurse will go over the discharge plan with you and your family or friend.12

Use the Hospital Plan of Care form to advocate and ask questions about your loved one.

The Hospital Plan of Care form

Questions to ask before the patient is discharged:

1. If the patient has a bandage or dressing, when will it need to be changed?189

2. Who does the patient call for help?189

3. Repeat back or "teach back" medication, treatment &/or self-care instructions to your doctor & nurse.10

Embedded content: https://youtu.be/MWUM7LIXDeA

4. Can I use my phone to record your discharge instructions?

5. Does my primary care doctor know about my admission to the hospital?189

6. Will a nurse or physical therapist visit the patient at home from a home health agency?189

7. Will the patient go home with medical equipment?189

8. Who will pay for this equipment?1

9. How much will the patient be responsible for the medical equipment?1

10. When can the patient shower or take a bath?189

11. You should have your follow up appointments scheduled before you leave the hospital189

12. Plan to have family and/or friends help you once you are home.1

13. How much physical activity can the patient do when home?18

14. If you or your loved one is going to a skilled nursing home or rehabilitation facility, how long will they be there?1

Skilled Nursing or Rehabilitation Facility Information from MedlinePlus

Discharge Medications:

1. Can you or the patient read the prescription?

2. Where does the patient get discharge medications?189

3. Ask for a drug information sheet when you get a new medication.

4. When do you take the first dose?

5. How often should this medication be taken?

6. How much should be taken?

7. What are the new medication side effects &/or possible interactions?

8. Will the patient need stool softeners or bowel medications if they are going home with narcotic pain medications?1

9. Are there any foods or drinks the patient can’t have when going home?189

10. Will any of the new medications interfere with medications the patient was taking before coming to hospital?1

11. Will the patient’s medication insurance pay for the new medications?1

12. Will the new medications need to be reviewed by the insurance plan?1

13. Call the Physician if you or your loved one has any of the following: 13456789

  • Shortness of breath, gasping, panting and/or fast breathing
  • Becomes very pale
  • Cold sweats, fever or chills
  • Confusion, dizziness, &/or acting strangely
  • Nausea and/or vomiting
  • Extreme pain
  • Headache not better after pain medication
  • Swelling of the face and hands
  • Sudden weight gain of 5 lbs in one week
  • Low urine or not having bowel movements
  • Oozing or pus from a wound or incision
  • Extra redness at wound or incision
  • Increased warmth at incision or wound
  • Seeing spots, flashing lights, blurring or unable to see from one eye or both
  • Yellowing of the skin
  • Bleeding from gums, nose, eyes or in urine &/or bowel movements
  • Seizures
